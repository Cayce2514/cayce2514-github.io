About

This page is a tribute to the life and accomplishments of Wendy Carlos. Information is sourced from her official biography page at www.wendycarlos.com and from Wikipedia.

Early Life

  • 1939 - On November 14th, Wendy Carlos was born as a boy, Walter Carlos in Pawtucket, Rhode Island.
  • 1945 - She started piano lessons at six years old and enjoyed art and science.
  • 1949 - She wrote her first composition, A Trio for Clarinet, Accordion, and Piano.
  • 1953 - She won a scholarship for a home-built computer at the Westinghouse Science Fair.
  • 1958 - 1962 - She studied at Brown University and graduated with a degree in music and physics. During her time at school, she taught lessons in electronic music at informal sessions.
  • 1965 - Wendy graduated with a master's degree in music composition from Columbia University.
  • 1960s - Wendy scored several films for students and a film maker from UNICEF. She also collaborated with Leonard Bernstein, Vladimir Ussachevsky and Otto Luening at the Columbia-Princeton Electronic Music Center in New York City.
  • 1968 -Wendy leaves her job as a recording and mastering engineer at Gotham Recording Studios in New York City.
  • 1966 - Wendy, through her friendship with Robert Moog becomes an owner of a small Moog sythesizer which she used to record sound effects and jingles for commercials.
  • 1967 - Wendy meets Rachel Elkind whom they became friends and shared a combination home, studio and business premises in NYC.

Personal Life

From an early age, around 5 or 6, Wendy Carlos, then Walter Carlos, felt as though she were a girl. She preferred to wear her hair long and wear girl's clothes. She didn't understand why her parents couldn't also see it.

After moving to New York, Wendy learned about the transgender phenomenon with now famous sexologist Harry Bengamin.

During her transition, she did not feel comfortable appearing in public as a woman. Though she was in the process of transitioning and had started hormones, she still chose to present herself as a Man.

  • 1968 - Wendy begins hormone therapy.
  • 1970 - Wendy appears on the Dick Cafett Show wearng a man's wig, fake sideburns and facial hair drawn on with an eyebrow pencil.
  • 1972 - Wendy received sex reassignment surgery, but still released her albums under Walter Carlos
  • 1979 - Wendy finally reveals herself as part of an interview with Playboy magazine.

Wendy is also a solar eclipse photographer. Her photographs were published by NASA and was on the cover of Sky & Telescope Magazine.
